Thomas J.Watson

Thomas John Watson Sr who was born on February 17, 1874 in Campbell, New York was the fifth child and only son of Thomas and Jane Fulton White. Watson was an American businessman who served as the chairman and CEO of IBM. Watson developed IBM's management style and corporate culture from John Henry Patterson's training at NCR.He turned the company into a highly effective selling organization, based largely on punched card tabulating machines
Thomas John Watson Sr was one of the richest men of his time and was called the world's greatest salesman when he died in 1956.


Facts of Thomas J.Watson

  • Watson became CEO in 1956, when his father retired
  • In 1968 Watson, together with his siblings, launched the fellowship program of the Thomas J. Watson Foundation, which his mother had established in 1961 in honour of her husband
  • Watson’s aggressive tactics and heavy outlays for research established IBM’s dominance in the industry so thoroughly that the U.S. government filed an antitrust suit against the company in 1969.
  • By the time Watson retired in 1971, IBM's stock had increased in value by more than $36 billion from when he gained the chairmanship
